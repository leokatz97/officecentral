Primary ICP — Central Ontario institutional / non-profit (~60% of online revenue): Administrative / operations purchaser at a non-profit, First Nations band office, family health team, small hospital, independent school, municipal office, or community services agency. Based in or near Peterborough, Kawartha Lakes, Northumberland, GTA, North Bay. Places small-to-mid orders (median $814, p75 $2,042), often repeat.
Secondary ICP — Ontario SMB private-sector (~40% of online revenue, higher AOV): Office manager, owner-operator, or ops lead at a professional services firm (law, insurance, accounting, medical, design), a manufacturer or industrial supplier, a construction/trades company, or a logistics/services business. Across Ontario. Orders span $500 replenishment to $10k+ multi-desk outfits.
Sub ICP — Rest of Canada excluding Quebec (~6% revenue, upside via SEO): Same two buyer archetypes in BC, Alberta, Manitoba, Saskatchewan, Atlantic Canada, and the territories. Treat as SEO upside, not a targeting rewrite. Quebec is excluded — language and logistics are a poor fit.

Voice Calibration Rules

Locked April 18, 2026 after reviewing 5 sample rewrites.

  • Phone CTA on every product. Close every description with Call 1-800-835-9565 and a soft ask. 20% of revenue flows through the quote channel.
  • Bold one-line hook as the opener. Every product starts with a use-case or promise sentence. Not a caption, not a spec.
  • "Who it's for" paragraph wherever there's a clear archetype. Apply to task chairs, executive chairs, bariatric seating, training tables, reception desks, and SKUs with an obvious buyer type.
  • Lead-time language: never commit to specific windows. Use ships from Ontario stock, Quick Ship item, or push to the phone. Do not write "5–10 business days" or any concrete number.
  • Warmth dial: confident, not lifestyle. "The chair that keeps your back where it should be" ✓ — "Elevate your workday" ✗
  • Delivery language: safe defaults. Delivery across Ontario and installation available are fine. Do not promise specific carriers, dates, or free shipping unless confirmed per-SKU.
  • Keep model codes in product titles. Format as Product Name Code or Product Name — Code. B2B reorders and procurement teams key off the code.

Voice tilts for Secondary ICP

  • High-ticket SKUs (multi-person workstations, executive suites, boardroom tables): lean into project partnership language. End with: "Planning a full office refresh? Call us at 1-800-835-9565 for free design layouts and contract pricing."
  • Professional-services SKUs (executive chairs, reception desks, leather seating): lean slightly more aesthetic + client-facing — "the kind of chair your clients sit across from when you close the deal."

Sub ICP — Rest of Canada (excluding Quebec)

Sub ICP · ~6% revenue · SEO upside

Same two buyer archetypes as Primary + Secondary, just outside Ontario. Treat as SEO upside, not a targeting rewrite. Quebec is excluded — language requirements (Loi 96) and freight/install logistics.

  • Lean on generic-Canada head terms, not Ontario-specific ones
  • Use ships across Canada alongside ships from Ontario stock
  • Installation language stays Ontario-only: installation available in Ontario
  • Do not build per-province landing pages until revenue justifies it

Who We Are NOT Targeting

  • ✗Home office / WFH consumers. No consumer price-point product, no 2-day shipping promise. Don't chase "home office desk under $500" keywords.
  • ✗Gamers / streamers. Different catalog, different voice, different brand.
  • ✗Enterprise / Fortune-500 procurement. They go to Steelcase/Herman Miller direct or national dealer networks.
  • ✗Design/architect specifiers sourcing unbranded product. Distant tertiary — maybe worth one landing page later.
  • ✗Quebec buyers. Language requirements and freight/install logistics make it the wrong fit. Don't chase Quebec keywords.

How to Use This Doc in SEO / Rewrite Sessions

  • For every product description: picture the Primary ICP reading it. Would the office administrator at a Peterborough community-services non-profit find the first sentence useful?
  • Meta titles: include product type + modifier + Canada/Ontario qualifier. Do not lead with "Brantford" — the actual customer base is not Brantford-local.
  • Category pages: write for the head term with a short intro in design-forward premium voice, and surface "Free Design Layouts" + "Delivery Across Ontario" as trust anchors.
  • High-ticket SKUs: write for the Secondary ICP's fit-out project mindset — end with a quote-channel CTA.
  • Institutional-leaning SKUs (bariatric, daycare, student chairs, healthcare seating): lean on durability + compliance + "built for high-traffic spaces."
  • Regional SEO pages: prioritise Peterborough, Toronto/GTA, Kawartha Lakes, Northumberland, Durham Region. A Brantford page is worth keeping for brand-name searches only.
  • National SEO: generic-Canada head terms only. Use ships across Canada + installation available in Ontario on heavy SKUs.
