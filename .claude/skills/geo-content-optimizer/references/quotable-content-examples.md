# Quotable Content Examples

Before/after examples of content optimized for AI citation. Each shows the transformation from generic to quotable.

## Example 1: Definition Block

**Before** (Citation: 1/10):
> SEO is really important for businesses and involves various techniques to improve visibility online through search engines.

**After** (Citation: 9/10):
> **Search Engine Optimization (SEO)** is a digital marketing practice that improves website rankings in organic search results through content optimization, technical improvements, and authority building. According to BrightEdge research, organic search drives 53% of all website traffic, making SEO the highest-impact channel for sustainable online visibility.

**Key fixes**: Precise terms, clear classification, authoritative stat with source, standalone statement.

## Example 3: Process/How-To Content

**Before** (Citation: 2/10):
> To do keyword research, you should think about what your customers might search for and then use some tools to find more keywords.

**After** (Citation: 8/10):
> To conduct effective keyword research:
>
> 1. **Identify seed keywords** - List 5-10 core topics your target audience searches for
> 2. **Expand with research tools** - Use Google Keyword Planner or Ahrefs to generate 100+ related keywords
> 3. **Analyze search intent** - Categorize each keyword as informational, commercial, or transactional
> 4. **Assess competition** - Evaluate ranking difficulty using domain authority and SERP analysis
> 5. **Prioritize strategically** - Select 10-15 keywords balancing volume (1,000+ monthly) with achievable competition
>
> This process typically takes 2-4 hours for a comprehensive initial keyword list.

**Key fixes**: Numbered steps, specific actions, concrete numbers and tools, time estimate.

## Example 4: Comparison Content

**Before** (Citation: 2/10):
> WordPress and Shopify are both popular website builders. WordPress is more flexible while Shopify is easier to use.

**After** (Citation: 9/10):
> | Factor | WordPress | Shopify |
> |--------|-----------|---------|
> | **Best for** | Content-heavy sites, blogs, custom needs | E-commerce, quick setup |
> | **Setup time** | 4-8 hours | 1-2 hours |
> | **Monthly cost** | $10-50 (hosting + theme) | $29-299 (subscription) |
> | **Customization** | Unlimited (50,000+ plugins) | Limited to Shopify apps |

> **Choose WordPress if**: You need maximum flexibility or run a content-first site.
> **Choose Shopify if**: E-commerce is your primary goal or you want fast setup.

**Key fixes**: Structured table, specific metrics, clear recommendations, quantifiable differences.

## Example 6: Q&A Content

**Before** (Citation: 1/10):
> **How long does SEO take?** It depends on a lot of factors. Sometimes it's fast, sometimes it takes a while.

**After** (Citation: 9/10):
> **How long does SEO take to show results?**
>
> SEO typically takes 3-6 months for new websites. Established sites may see improvements in 1-3 months. Results depend on four key factors:
>
> 1. **Domain authority** - New domains: 6-12 months; established: faster results
> 2. **Competition level** - Low-competition: 1-3 months; high-competition: 12+ months
> 3. **Implementation quality** - Comprehensive optimization accelerates results
> 4. **Backlink profile** - Sites with 20+ quality backlinks see 40% faster improvements (Ahrefs study)
>
> Budget 6 months for measurable ROI from SEO efforts.

**Key fixes**: Specific timeframes, factors explained, differentiated by scenario, supporting data.

## Example 7: List Content

**Before** (Citation: 2/10):
> Important on-page SEO factors: Title tags, Meta descriptions, Headers, Content, Links, Images

**After** (Citation: 8/10):
> Critical on-page SEO factors ranked by impact:
>
> 1. **Title tags** - Most important on-page element; include primary keyword within first 60 characters
> 2. **Content quality and depth** - Comprehensive content (1,500+ words for competitive keywords) outranks thin content
> 3. **Header structure (H1-H6)** - Use one H1 with primary keyword, multiple H2s for sections
> 4. **Internal linking** - Link to 3-5 related pages using descriptive anchor text
> 5. **Image optimization** - Compress to under 100KB, use descriptive alt text
> 6. **URL structure** - Use descriptive, keyword-rich URLs; clean URLs improve CTR by 25% (Backlinko)

**Key fixes**: Prioritized ranking, specific guidance per item, actionable recommendations, supporting data.

## Example 9: Technical Explanation

**Before** (Citation: 2/10):
> Core Web Vitals are metrics that Google uses to measure user experience. You should try to improve them.

**After** (Citation: 9/10):
> Core Web Vitals are three metrics Google uses to measure page experience:
>
> | Metric | Measures | Good Score |
> |--------|----------|------------|
> | **LCP** | Loading performance | <2.5 seconds |
> | **FID** | Interactivity | <100 milliseconds |
> | **CLS** | Visual stability | <0.1 |
>
> Pages meeting all three thresholds rank higher and receive 24% more clicks (Google 2024). Core Web Vitals became an official ranking factor in June 2021.

**Key fixes**: Specific metrics with thresholds, table format, official data with source.

## Transformation Checklist

| Content Type | Key Requirements |
|-------------|-----------------|
| Definitions | Precise terms, category classification, standalone, include scope |
| Statistics | Specific numbers, source + year, context, verifiable |
| Processes | Numbered steps, actionable, time estimates, specific tools |
| Comparisons | Table format, comparable metrics, "choose if..." recommendations |
| Lists | Prioritized, explained, specific guidance, examples |
| Expert content | Attributed quotes, credentials, research citations |

## Quick Quotability Test

Content is highly quotable (8+/10 yes) when it:
1. Can be quoted without additional context
2. Includes specific numbers
3. Has clear source attribution
4. Uses precise, unambiguous language
5. Would pass expert review
6. Is scannable (structured)
7. Is up-to-date
8. Can be verified
9. Is specific to a use case
10. Answers a complete question
