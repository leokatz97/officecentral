# Brant Business Interiors / Office Central — ICP

**Purpose:** Lock in the target customer before rewriting ~200 product descriptions or running SEO. Every copy and keyword decision below should ladder back to this doc.

**Date:** 2026-04-18
**Store:** office-central-online.myshopify.com
**Working dir:** `/Users/leokatz/Desktop/Office Central/`

**Scope decisions (confirmed with Leo, 2026-04-18; geography updated 2026-04-19):**
- Geography: **Ontario is the prime ICP** for SEO and ICP targeting. **Rest of Canada (excluding Quebec) is a sub ICP** — worth chasing with generic-Canada keywords and bilingual-free copy, but not the lead focus. Quebec is explicitly out of scope (language + logistics). Non-Canadian orders welcome but not chased.
- Budget frame for copy & keywords: **$500–$15k**. Real median is $814 and p75 is $2k — most copy should feel at home in that zone, with project-oriented copy kicking in for $5k+ SKUs.
- Indigenous / First Nations buyers are real revenue (~17% of named-company orders) but **not featured** in positioning, voice, or keyword targeting. Treat as background evidence, not a segment we market to.

**Voice calibration (locked 2026-04-18, after reviewing 5 sample rewrites):**
- **Phone CTA on every product.** Close every description with `Call **1-800-835-9565**` and a soft ask. 20% of revenue flows through the quote channel — keep the number visible.
- **Bold one-line hook as the opener.** Every product starts with a `**Use-case or promise.**` sentence. Not a caption, not a spec.
- **"Who it's for" paragraph wherever there's a clear archetype.** Apply to task chairs, executive chairs, bariatric seating, training tables, reception desks, and any SKU with an obvious buyer type. Skip where the use case is obvious from the opening hook.
- **Lead-time language: never commit to specific day/week windows.** Use `ships from Ontario stock`, `Quick Ship item`, or push buyers to the phone for lead times. **Do not write** "5–10 business days," "4–6 weeks," or any concrete number — we can't verify those across the catalog. If a lead-time claim feels important, the fallback is always: *"Call 1-800-835-9565 for current lead times."*
- **Warmth dial: confident, not lifestyle.** The 5-sample voice is the reference. Openers like "The chair that keeps your back where it should be" are in. Openers like "Elevate your workday" are out.
- **Delivery language: safe defaults.** `Delivery across Ontario` and `installation available` are fine. Do not promise specific carriers, dates, or free shipping unless confirmed per-SKU.
- **Product names: keep the product/model code in the title.** Do not strip SKU codes, model numbers, or series identifiers (e.g. `1240-3`, `G20`, `Premium Series`) when rewriting product names. A meaningful share of buyers search by code — reorders, spec-sheet lookups, and procurement teams matching a PO all key off the code. Cleaner-looking names without the code lose that traffic. Format as `<Product Name> <Code>` or `<Product Name> — <Code>`; don't hide codes in the body only.

## TL;DR — Grounded in order data (235 orders, Oct 2024–Apr 2026, 134 named companies)

**The online store serves BOTH institutional and SMB private-sector buyers — roughly 60/40 by revenue after correcting for classification gaps.** Two ICPs, both online, both worth writing for. The 20% quote channel sits on top of this for the very largest fit-outs, not as a separate ICP.

> **Primary ICP — Central Ontario institutional / non-profit (~60% of online revenue):** Administrative / operations purchaser at a non-profit, First Nations band office, family health team, small hospital, independent school, municipal office, or community services agency. Based in or near **Peterborough, Kawartha Lakes, Northumberland, GTA, North Bay**. Places small-to-mid orders (median $814, p75 $2,042), often repeat.

> **Secondary ICP — Ontario SMB private-sector (~40% of online revenue, higher AOV):** Office manager, owner-operator, or ops lead at a professional services firm (law, insurance, accounting, medical, design), a manufacturer or industrial supplier, a construction/trades company, or a logistics/services business. Across Ontario (Peterborough + Toronto + Mississauga + Barrie + smaller towns). Orders span $500 replenishment to $10k+ multi-desk outfits — and the biggest ones (>$15k) **do** escalate to the phone/quote channel but often start on the site.

> **Sub ICP — Rest of Canada excluding Quebec (~6% of current revenue, upside via SEO):** Same two buyer archetypes (institutional/non-profit and SMB private-sector) in BC, Alberta, Manitoba, Saskatchewan, Atlantic Canada, and the territories. Small current share (BC $7k, Manitoba $10k, Alberta $5k) but the catalog ships nationally and the "Canadian office furniture supplier" positioning travels. Worth capturing via generic-Canada keywords and national long-tail. **Quebec is excluded** — language and logistics make it a poor fit.

> **Tone:** **Design-forward & premium** (Leo's direction) — but grounded. For Primary: lean durability + "works in a busy front-of-house / classroom / clinic" + PO-friendly + Canadian supplier trust. For Secondary: lean craft + commercial-grade + workspace-fit + optional "call us for a free design layout." Avoid abstract lifestyle fluff either way. Think "thoughtfully designed for the way you actually work" not "unleash your potential" and not "SKU 4382 has a 5-year limited warranty on the base."

### Tone & voice — Primary

Per Leo: **Design-forward & premium.**

**Lead with:**
1. **The use case / workspace feeling** — "Built for focused solo work with a clear visual anchor for your space," not "This desk has 4 legs and measures 60x30."
2. **Craft + materials** — finishes, edge profile, hardware, mesh tension, cable management — but in human language
3. **Commercial-grade reassurance** — warranty, contract-grade build, Business Interiors / Global Furniture Group pedigree
4. **Canadian + Ontario + designed-for-your-space** — free design layouts, sales humans, 5 Ontario locations

**Sentence structure:** Short, confident, benefit-first. Spec table lives below the paragraph, not inside it.

**Don't:**
- Don't shout specs in the first sentence
- Don't use manufacturer boilerplate ("Category: Task Seating / Manufacturer: Global / Mfr Ref: ...") — this is what 203 of the 650 current descriptions look like and it's exactly what we're replacing

- Don't use US-English defaults — it's `centre`, `colour`, `metre` (matches .ca audience)

**Sample opening line, right tone:**

> "A clean, L-shaped desk that anchors a private office or director's workspace — built on a commercial-grade steel frame with a wood-grain laminate top, designed to take a full workday and a decade of them."

**Sample opening line, wrong tone (too spec-forward):**

## Who we are NOT targeting

Being explicit saves rewrite cycles:

- ❌ **Home office / WFH consumers.** No consumer-price-point product (<$100 is delivery/install fees), no consumer support ops, no 2-day shipping promise. Don't chase `home office desk under $500` type keywords.
- ❌ **Gamers / streamers.** Different catalog, different voice, different brand.
- ❌ **Enterprise / Fortune-500 procurement.** Scale and RFP process don't match — they go to Steelcase/Herman Miller direct or to national dealer networks, not a 5-location Ontario shop.
- ❌ **Design/architect specifiers sourcing unbranded product** (a distant tertiary — maybe worth one landing page later, not a catalog-wide rewrite target).
- ❌ **Quebec buyers.** Language (French-language packaging, descriptions, and customer service expectations under Loi 96) and freight/install logistics make it the wrong fit. Orders welcome if they come in, but don't write for this audience and don't chase Quebec keywords.

## How to use this doc in the SEO / rewrite session

- For every product description: picture the **Primary ICP** reading it. Would the office administrator at a Peterborough community-services non-profit reordering a task chair find the first sentence useful? Would a Kawartha Health Team ordering a waiting-room chair feel confident it's durable enough?
- **Meta titles:** include product type + modifier + Canada/Ontario qualifier (e.g. `Ergonomic Mesh Task Chair | Commercial Office Furniture Canada | Brant Business Interiors`). **Do not lead with "Brantford"** — the actual customer base is not Brantford-local.
- **Category/collection pages:** write for the head term (e.g. "Office Chairs Canada") with a short intro paragraph in the design-forward premium voice, and surface "Free Design Layouts" + "Delivery Across Ontario" as trust anchors.
- **High-ticket SKUs** (multi-person workstations, boardroom tables, executive suites): write for the Secondary ICP's fit-out project mindset — end with a quote-channel CTA.
- **Institutional-leaning SKUs** (bariatric, daycare, student chairs, healthcare seating): already aligned with Primary ICP — lean slightly more on durability + compliance + "built for high-traffic spaces."
- If a SKU serves both ICPs (e.g. a heavy-duty task chair that fits a community centre AND a law firm), write for Primary and add a secondary-use line in the body.
- **Regional SEO pages:** consider dedicated landing pages for **Peterborough, Toronto/GTA, Kawartha Lakes, Northumberland, Durham Region** — these match the real customer geography. A Brantford page is worth keeping for brand-name searches but should not be the primary regional SEO target.
- **National SEO (Sub ICP):** lean on **generic-Canada head terms** (`office chair Canada`, `commercial office furniture Canada`, `Canadian office furniture supplier`) rather than building out per-province landing pages. Ensure product copy doesn't box us into Ontario-only delivery language — use `ships across Canada` + `installation available in Ontario` on heavy SKUs. **Do not build Quebec pages or French copy** — Quebec is out of scope.
