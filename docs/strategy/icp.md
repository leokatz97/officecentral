**Scope decisions (confirmed with Leo, 2026-04-18; segment weighting + geography + buying mode updated with Steve, 2026-05-06):**
- **Segment weighting:** Institutional / non-profit and SMB private-sector are **co-primary, equal-weight**. Both ICPs get full attention in copy, voice anchors, keyword targeting, and page builds. Neither is "secondary."
- **Geography:** **Ontario AND Western Canada (BC, Alberta, Saskatchewan, Manitoba) are co-primary** for SEO, ICP targeting, and regional landing pages. Atlantic Canada + territories are sub-ICP — capture with generic-Canada keywords, no dedicated regional pages yet. Quebec is explicitly out of scope (language + logistics). Non-Canadian orders welcome but not chased.
- **Buying mode:** The site serves **two equally valid flows** — (a) direct online checkout for quick-ship standard SKUs, and (b) the quote channel for project-scale fit-outs, custom-config orders, and bulk purchases. Copy should never funnel everything into "Request a Quote." Buyers who arrive ready to order a chair or a few desks should be able to add to cart and check out without friction. Buyers planning a full office should be guided to the quote channel.
- **Install:** **Installation available in Ontario AND Western Canada** (BC, AB, SK, MB). Atlantic Canada + territories: delivery only — call for freight quote.
- **OECM:** **Ontario-only as a procurement vehicle** (it's the Ontario broader-public-sector buying group). Mentionable site-wide as a trust signal — *"we hold the OECM Supplier Partner agreement, the most rigorous Ontario institutional procurement standard, and apply that quality bar across our catalog"* — but never positioned as a buying mechanism for non-Ontario buyers.
- **Budget frame for copy & keywords:** **$500–$15k**. Real median is $814 and p75 is $2k — most copy should feel at home in that zone, with project-oriented copy kicking in for $5k+ SKUs.
- Indigenous / First Nations buyers are real revenue (~17% of named-company orders) but **not featured** in positioning, voice, or keyword targeting. Treat as background evidence, not a segment we market to.

**Voice calibration (locked 2026-04-18, after reviewing 5 sample rewrites; dual-CTA + national delivery defaults added 2026-05-06):**
- **Phone CTA on every product.** Close every description with `Call **1-800-835-9565**` and a soft ask. ~20% of revenue flows through the quote channel — keep the number visible.
- **Dual CTA on quick-ship SKUs.** Standard quick-ship products (single chairs, single desks, accessories, replacement parts, anything shippable from existing stock with no custom config) lead with `Add to cart` and surface `Call **1-800-835-9565**` as the secondary action. Project-scale SKUs (multi-person workstations, custom-config systems, boardroom packages, COM upholstery, anything that needs a layout) lead with `Request a Quote` and the phone number; cart is secondary or absent. **Do not force a quote-only path on items the buyer can order in one click.**
- **Bold one-line hook as the opener.** Every product starts with a `**Use-case or promise.**` sentence. Not a caption, not a spec.
- **"Who it's for" paragraph wherever there's a clear archetype.** Apply to task chairs, executive chairs, bariatric seating, training tables, reception desks, and any SKU with an obvious buyer type. Skip where the use case is obvious from the opening hook.
- **Lead-time language: never commit to specific day/week windows.** Use `ships from Ontario stock`, `Quick Ship item`, `ships across Canada`, or push buyers to the phone for lead times. **Do not write** "5–10 business days," "4–6 weeks," or any concrete number — we can't verify those across the catalog. If a lead-time claim feels important, the fallback is always: *"Call 1-800-835-9565 for current lead times."*
- **Warmth dial: confident, not lifestyle.** The 5-sample voice is the reference. Openers like "The chair that keeps your back where it should be" are in. Openers like "Elevate your workday" are out.
- **Delivery language: safe defaults.** `Delivery across Canada` and `installation available in Ontario and Western Canada` are the new safe defaults. Atlantic Canada + territories: `delivery available — call for a freight quote`. Do not promise specific carriers, dates, or free shipping unless confirmed per-SKU.
- **Product names: keep the product/model code in the title.** Do not strip SKU codes, model numbers, or series identifiers (e.g. `1240-3`, `G20`, `Premium Series`) when rewriting product names. A meaningful share of buyers search by code — reorders, spec-sheet lookups, and procurement teams matching a PO all key off the code. Cleaner-looking names without the code lose that traffic. Format as `<Product Name> <Code>` or `<Product Name> — <Code>`; don't hide codes in the body only.

## TL;DR — Co-primary ICPs, two geographies, two buying modes

**The online store serves two co-primary buyer types — institutional / non-profit and SMB private-sector — across two co-primary geographies: Ontario and Western Canada (BC, AB, SK, MB).** Equal weight in copy, voice anchors, keywords, and page builds. Atlantic Canada + territories are sub-ICP (national long-tail SEO captures them; no dedicated regional pages yet). Quebec is excluded.

The site also serves **two equally valid buying modes**: (a) direct online checkout for quick-ship standard SKUs, and (b) the quote channel for project-scale fit-outs, custom-config orders, and bulk purchases. Roughly 80% of orders flow self-serve through the site today; the ~20% that go through the quote channel are typically the larger fit-outs. Copy should support both — never bury the cart on items the buyer can order in one click, never bury the phone on items that need a real conversation.

> **Primary ICP A — Institutional / non-profit (Ontario + Western Canada):** Administrative / operations purchaser at a non-profit, family health team, small hospital, independent school, municipal office, community services agency, or band office. Ontario hubs: Peterborough, Kawartha Lakes, Northumberland, GTA, North Bay. Western Canada targets: Vancouver / Lower Mainland, Victoria, Calgary, Edmonton, Winnipeg, Saskatoon, Regina. Places small-to-mid orders (median $814, p75 $2,042), often repeat. **Note on procurement:** OECM is Ontario-specific. For Western institutional buyers, OECM appears as a trust signal — "the most rigorous Ontario institutional procurement standard, applied across our catalog" — not as a purchase mechanism. Western institutional buyers use their own vehicles (BC Bid, Alberta Purchasing Connection, MERX nationally) — we don't position around those, we just don't claim OECM does the work for them.

> **Primary ICP B — SMB private-sector (Ontario + Western Canada):** Office manager, owner-operator, ops lead, or practice manager at a professional services firm (law, insurance, accounting, medical, design), a manufacturer or industrial supplier, a construction/trades company, or a logistics/services business. Ontario hubs: Peterborough, Toronto, Mississauga, Markham, Barrie. Western Canada hubs: Vancouver / Lower Mainland (Burnaby, Surrey, Richmond), Victoria, Calgary, Edmonton, Winnipeg. Orders span $500 replenishment to $10k+ multi-desk outfits; the biggest ones (>$15k) escalate to phone/quote but often start on the site.

> **Sub ICP — Atlantic Canada + territories:** Same two buyer archetypes, smaller volume. Capture via generic-Canada keywords and national long-tail SEO. Delivery only (no install). No dedicated regional pages until revenue justifies. **Quebec remains excluded** — language and logistics make it a poor fit.

> **Tone:** **Design-forward & premium** — but grounded. For both ICPs, lean durability + commercial-grade build + Canadian supplier trust + workspace-fit. Institutional copy adds: PO-friendly + "works in a busy front-of-house / classroom / clinic." SMB copy adds: craft + client-facing aesthetic + optional "call us for a free design layout." Avoid abstract lifestyle fluff either way. Think "thoughtfully designed for the way you actually work" not "unleash your potential" and not "SKU 4382 has a 5-year limited warranty on the base."

## Buying modes — direct cart vs. quote channel

The catalog supports two distinct purchase paths and copy must serve both without forcing one into the other.

| Mode | Best for | What the page should do |
|---|---|---|
| **Direct online checkout (cart)** | Quick-ship standard SKUs: single chairs, single desks, accessories, replacement parts, small orders (1–3 units of an in-stock item), reorders by name | Lead with `Add to cart`. Show price clearly. Surface `Call 1-800-835-9565` as the secondary CTA for buyers who'd rather talk. Quote CTA can sit lower on the page or in the hero secondary slot — never the only path. |
| **Quote channel** | Project-scale fit-outs, multi-person workstations, custom-config systems (boardroom packages, COM upholstery), bulk orders (10+ chairs of one model), anything needing a layout / design service | Lead with `Request a Quote` + the phone number. Cart absent or de-emphasized. Surface "free design layout" as part of the value prop. |

**SKU classification** (used by content briefs and the `bbi-build-page` skill):

- **Quick-ship SKU:** Single-unit standard product, no configuration required, ships from existing Ontario or Western Canada stock. Examples: ObusForme Comfort 1240-3 chair, single L-shaped desk, lateral file cabinet (single drawer count), training table (single size), accessories.
- **Project SKU:** Multi-unit or multi-component, custom configuration, design layout helpful or required. Examples: multi-person workstation systems, boardroom table + chair packages, COM-upholstered seating, full executive suites, anything where the buyer needs to specify a fabric / finish / layout.
- **Both:** Some SKUs serve both modes (e.g. a popular task chair sold one-at-a-time AND in 50-unit fleet orders). Page lead: cart for the single-unit case; surface quote prominently as "ordering 10+? Call us for fleet pricing."

**Why this matters:** ~80% of order flow today is self-serve checkout. Burying the cart behind quote-only language costs us those buyers — the office manager who wanted to reorder one ObusForme chair shouldn't have to call. At the same time, ~20% of revenue is the quote channel, and project-scale buyers need the design service positioned as the value, not the friction.

### Tone & voice — ICP A

Per Leo: **Design-forward & premium.**

**Lead with:**
1. **The use case / workspace feeling** — "Built for focused solo work with a clear visual anchor for your space," not "This desk has 4 legs and measures 60x30."
2. **Craft + materials** — finishes, edge profile, hardware, mesh tension, cable management — but in human language
3. **Commercial-grade reassurance** — warranty, contract-grade build, Business Interiors / Global Furniture Group pedigree
4. **Canadian + designed-for-your-space** — free design layouts, sales humans, Ontario + Western Canada delivery and install
5. **OECM as quality benchmark** (not as procurement vehicle for Western buyers)

**Sentence structure:** Short, confident, benefit-first. Spec table lives below the paragraph, not inside it.

**Don't:**
- Don't shout specs in the first sentence
- Don't use manufacturer boilerplate ("Category: Task Seating / Manufacturer: Global / Mfr Ref: ...") — this is what 203 of the 650 current descriptions look like and it's exactly what we're replacing
- Don't go flowery-lifestyle either — these are buyers, not browsers. "Thoughtfully designed" yes, "unleash your potential" no
- Don't use US-English defaults — it's `centre`, `colour`, `metre` (matches .ca audience)
- Don't claim OECM solves Western Canada procurement — it doesn't

**Sample opening line, right tone:**

> "A clean, L-shaped desk that anchors a private office or director's workspace — built on a commercial-grade steel frame with a wood-grain laminate top, designed to take a full workday and a decade of them."

**Sample opening line, wrong tone (too spec-forward):**

> "The L-Shaped Desk with Single Pedestal features a 72" x 30" work surface with return, constructed of 1" thermofused laminate with 3mm PVC edge banding..."

**Sample opening line, wrong tone (too flowery):**

> "Elevate your workday with the desk that believes in you. Dream bigger. Work bolder."

## Who we are NOT targeting

Being explicit saves rewrite cycles:

- ❌ **Home office / WFH consumers.** No consumer-price-point product (<$100 is delivery/install fees), no consumer support ops, no 2-day shipping promise. Don't chase `home office desk under $500` type keywords.
- ❌ **Gamers / streamers.** Different catalog, different voice, different brand.
- ❌ **Enterprise / Fortune-500 procurement.** Scale and RFP process don't match — they go to Steelcase/Herman Miller direct or to national dealer networks.
- ❌ **Design/architect specifiers sourcing unbranded product** (a distant tertiary — maybe worth one landing page later, not a catalog-wide rewrite target).
- ❌ **Quebec buyers.** Language (French-language packaging, descriptions, and customer service expectations under Loi 96) and freight/install logistics make it the wrong fit. Orders welcome if they come in, but don't write for this audience and don't chase Quebec keywords.

## How to use this doc in the SEO / rewrite session

- For every product description: picture **either ICP A or ICP B** reading it (or both, if the SKU serves both). Would the office administrator at a Peterborough community-services non-profit reordering a task chair find the first sentence useful? Would the office manager at a Calgary law firm shopping for a new boardroom table feel confident it's commercial-grade?
- **Decide cart vs quote first.** Before drafting copy, classify the SKU as quick-ship (cart-led), project (quote-led), or both. The CTA structure flows from there.
- **Meta titles:** include product type + modifier + Canada/Ontario/regional qualifier (e.g. `Ergonomic Mesh Task Chair | Commercial Office Furniture Canada | Brant Business Interiors`). **Do not lead with "Brantford"** — the actual customer base is not Brantford-local.
- **Category/collection pages:** write for the head term (e.g. "Office Chairs Canada") with a short intro paragraph in the design-forward premium voice, and surface "Free Design Layouts" + "Delivery across Canada" + "Install in Ontario and Western Canada" as trust anchors.
- **High-ticket SKUs** (multi-person workstations, boardroom tables, executive suites): write for ICP B's fit-out project mindset — end with a quote-channel CTA. Cart de-emphasized.
- **Quick-ship SKUs** (single chairs, single desks, accessories): write for the buyer who's ready to order — lead with cart, surface phone as secondary.
- **Institutional-leaning SKUs** (bariatric, daycare, student chairs, healthcare seating): aligned with ICP A — lean slightly more on durability + compliance + "built for high-traffic spaces."
- If a SKU serves both ICPs (e.g. a heavy-duty task chair that fits a community centre AND a law firm), write the body for ICP A and add a secondary-use line for ICP B.
- **Regional SEO pages:** consider dedicated landing pages for **Ontario:** Peterborough, Toronto/GTA, Kawartha Lakes, Northumberland, Durham Region. **Western Canada:** Vancouver, Calgary, Edmonton, Winnipeg as the first wave; Victoria, Saskatoon, Regina as a second wave once first-wave traffic justifies. A Brantford page is worth keeping for brand-name searches but should not be the primary regional SEO target.
- **National SEO (Sub ICP):** lean on **generic-Canada head terms** (`office chair Canada`, `commercial office furniture Canada`, `Canadian office furniture supplier`) to capture Atlantic + territories. **Do not build Quebec pages or French copy** — Quebec is out of scope.
- **OECM:** lead with OECM on Ontario institutional landing pages and any Ontario broader-public-sector vertical (healthcare, education, government, non-profit). On Western Canada landing pages, OECM is mentionable as a quality benchmark but never as a procurement vehicle.

**Quick-ship vs project SKU determination** (used by content briefs and the `bbi-build-page` skill):

- **Quick-ship SKU:** Single-unit standard product, no configuration required, ships from existing Ontario or Western Canada stock. Examples: ObusForme Comfort 1240-3 chair, single L-shaped desk, lateral file cabinet (single drawer count), training table (single size), accessories, replacement parts.
- **Project SKU:** Multi-unit, multi-component, or custom-configured. Design layout helpful or required. Examples: multi-person workstation systems, boardroom table + chair packages, COM-upholstered seating, full executive suites, anything where the buyer specifies fabric / finish / layout.
- **Both (dual-CTA):** Some SKUs serve both modes (e.g. a popular task chair sold one-at-a-time AND in 50-unit fleet orders). Lead: cart for the single-unit case; surface quote prominently as "ordering 10+? Call us for fleet pricing."

**Voice anchors per ICP** (apply on top of the locked microcopy above):

| Anchor | Primary ICP A (institutional, ON + Western Canada) | Primary ICP B (SMB private-sector, ON + Western Canada) |
|---|---|---|
| Headline tone | Outcome + procurement-grade trust | Outcome + speed-of-quote (or speed-of-checkout for cart-led) |
| Subheading anchor | OECM line (Ontario) / "Designed to OECM standard" (Western) + phone | "Free space plan included" + phone (project SKUs); price + cart (quick-ship SKUs) |
| Body voice | Direct, fact-led, formal-but-warm | Direct, fact-led, slightly more conversational |
| Forbidden words | "innovative", "cutting-edge", "synergy", "world-class", "best-in-class", "leverage" (verb), "solutions" (as noun fluff) | Same forbidden list applies |
| CTA voice | Imperative ("Request a Quote", "Call 1-800-835-9565", "Add to cart") | Same imperative voice |
| Pronouns | "We" for BBI, "you" for the buyer, "your team / your space / your school / your facility" | "We" for BBI, "you" for the buyer, "your office / your team / your space" |

**Why locked here, not just in the skill:** these rules apply outside `/bbi-build-page` too — email templates, sales follow-ups, product descriptions, blog posts, ad copy. Anyone writing customer-facing BBI copy reads this section first.
