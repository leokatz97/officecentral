  • AI search infrastructure — the six things missing from most Shopify sites that LLMs actually use to decide who to cite

Tier 6

Increase Repeat Purchases (17% → Target 30%)

Every 1% is nearly free revenue. Top 10 customers = 30% of revenue — protect them.

  • Non-completion email flows — recover the people who didn't finish — Shopify already captures the email on most of these events. Set up a distinct automated sequence for each drop-off point, because they mean different things and need different asks:
    • Abandoned cart — added to cart, never checked out. 3-email series over 7 days:
      1. +1 hour — "You left this in your cart" — product image, one-click return-to-cart link, trust signals (30-year-old Canadian business, free design layout)
      2. +24 hours — add social proof + objection handling ("not sure about delivery? we handle install across Ontario / we can also quote it if you need a PO")
      3. +72 hours — soft CTA: "Want a human to spec this for your space? Reply and we'll build a free layout." Route replies to Steve
    • Abandoned checkout — entered checkout, didn't pay. Treat separately from cart — intent is much higher. 2-email series:
      1. +30 min — "Need a PO number or invoice instead? Reply and we'll switch it to a quote" — the #1 reason B2B buyers bail at checkout is procurement red tape
      2. +24 hours — last-nudge return link + phone number
    • Abandoned quote request (B2B — highest value) — quote form submitted or quote emailed, no reply after N days. Auto-follow-up sequence off the quote (Tier 4):
      1. +3 days — "Did you get our quote? Any questions on specs or lead time?"
      2. +7 days — offer an alternative (cheaper SKU, in-stock swap, smaller package)
      3. +14 days — "Still timing this in? Happy to hold the pricing for 30 days"
      4. +30 days — "Closing this quote — want me to reopen it?" Keeps the door open without nagging
    • Browse abandonment — logged-in or identified visitor viewed a product / collection 2+ times but never added to cart. Single email at +48h: "Still looking at [product]? Here are 3 similar options + a free design layout offer." Only fire on high-intent pages (chairs, desks, boardroom tables), not blog or about
    • Post-quote-accepted no-order — quote was accepted or marked "approved by procurement" but order never placed. +5 days: "Ready to go? Reply with the PO and we'll kick off fulfillment." Route to Steve
    • Plumbing — all of the above run in Shopify Email or Klaviyo (Klaviyo is worth it once volume justifies — better B2B segmentation). Suppress anyone who's purchased or replied. Brand-match every template to the design system from Tier 3. Measure open / click / recovered-revenue per flow monthly
  • Post-purchase email at 30 days: "How's the install?"
  • Post-purchase email at 6 months: "Time to refresh?"
  • Post-purchase email at 12 months: "Growing team? Free design layout on us"
  • Build a "Procurement Account" login with saved addresses, POs, and invoices
  • Case studies with top 3 customers (with logo + permission) — used as trust content on the site, not outbound

How to Work Through This

The tier order above is the execution order. Work top to bottom. Don't jump ahead — each tier assumes the previous one is done.

Tiers that can safely run in parallel once their predecessors are complete: Tier 5 + Tier 6 (SEO and retention emails don't depend on each other), and Tier 8 + Tier 9 (trust polish and automation are independent finishing work). Everything else is strictly sequential.
